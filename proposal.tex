\section{Problem}
The goal of our project is to create a playable simulation of a computer game by using a diffusion model. For such a model, the input is the previous frame (or a sequence of frames) and the user action. The output is the next frame. Various computer games have already been simulated using this approach. We plan on simulating a retro platformer, e.g. Super Mario Brothers (1985).

\section{Data}
It is our intention to generate our own trainig data using pre-existing games. This data will be formatted as a sequence of frames and actions performed during those frames. To capture this data we can use a reinforcement learning platform such as Gymnasium \footnote{https://openreview.net/pdf?id=qPMLvJxtPK}, which will allow us to automatically generate a large amount of data.


\section{Approach and Experiments}

We have drawn inspiration from \textit{GameNGen}\footnote{https://arxiv.org/pdf/2408.14837} and \textit{Oasis}\footnote{https://oasis-model.github.io/}, simulating Minecraft and Doom respectively. Both comprise of an auto-encoder and a diffusion model. The former is based on pre-trained \textit{Stable Diffusion 1.4} models while the latter uses a transformer-based architecture with \textit{ViT}\footnote{https://arxiv.org/pdf/2010.11929} and \textit{DiT}\footnote{https://arxiv.org/pdf/2212.09748}.

In Appendix A of \textit{GameNGen}, section 10, the authors describe their inital experiment on the dinosaur game present in Google Chrome web browser, noting that their approach worked well with only 2000 episodes of training data and 3000 training epochs. This shows us the feasibility of scaling down their approach. The fact that pre-trained models are used and only fine tuned is also beneficial.

\subsection{Experiments}

First experiments will involve finding the optimal architecture for the autoencoder itself. Since it is probable that the diffusion transformer will have multiple frames as input, making the encoded frames as small as possible will help with keeping the whole network lightweight. Experiments involving the diffusion transformer include trying different base models and experimenting with the amount of past frames to encode with the input.
